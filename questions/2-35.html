The Lebesgue integral of an indicator function 1_A with respect to measure $\mu$ equals:
Definitions: The indicator function $1_A$ (also written $\mathbb{1}_A$ or $\chi_A$) equals 1 on the set $A$ and 0 elsewhere. The Lebesgue integral with respect to a measure $\mu$ generalizes the notion of "summing up" values weighted by the measure.

Reasoning: By definition of the Lebesgue integral for simple functions, $\int_\Omega 1_A \, d\mu = 1 \cdot \mu(A) + 0 \cdot \mu(A^c) = \mu(A)$. This fundamental property shows that integration generalizes the measure: integrating an indicator gives the measure of the set. This is the building block for defining integrals of more complex functions.

Answer: The integral equals $\mu(A)$.
