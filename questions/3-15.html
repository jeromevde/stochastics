Definitions: In FX modeling, we have domestic and foreign risk-neutral measures $\mathbb{P}_\alpha$ and $\mathbb{P}_f$, corresponding to domestic and foreign bank accounts as numeraires. The FX rate $X(t)$ is the domestic currency price of one unit of foreign currency. Reasoning: The Radon-Nikodym derivative relating the foreign and domestic measures is $\frac{d\mathbb{P}_f}{d\mathbb{P}_\alpha}\Big|_{\mathcal{F}_t} = Y(t) = \frac{X(t) B_f(t)}{B_\alpha(t)}$, where $B_f$ and $B_\alpha$ are foreign and domestic bank accounts. This ratio represents the value in domestic currency of the foreign numeraire relative to the domestic numeraire. It's the key ingredient for switching between domestic and foreign risk-neutral measures. Answer: The Radon-Nikodym derivative is $Y(t) = X(t)B_f(t)/B_\alpha(t)$.
