A model is said to be 'arbitrage-free' if:
Definitions: An arbitrage is a self-financing trading strategy starting with zero capital that yields a non-negative payoff with positive probability of a strictly positive payoffâ€”essentially a "free lunch." A self-financing strategy means no external cash is added or removed after the initial investment.

Reasoning: A model is arbitrage-free if no such strategy exists. The formal definition: there is no self-financing strategy $(\phi_t)$ with zero initial cost such that the terminal value $V_T \geq 0$ almost surely and $P(V_T > 0) > 0$. By the First Fundamental Theorem of Asset Pricing, this is equivalent to the existence of an equivalent martingale measure.

Answer: A model is arbitrage-free if no self-financing strategy yields a certain positive profit from zero initial investment.
