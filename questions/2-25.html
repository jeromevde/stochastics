For 2D Brownian motion ($B_1$, $B_2$) with correlation $\rho$, the quadratic covariation [$B_1$, $B_2$]$_t$ equals:
Definitions: For two stochastic processes $X$ and $Y$, the quadratic covariation $[X, Y]_t$ is defined as $\lim_{\|\Pi\| \to 0} \sum_i (X(t_i) - X(t_{i-1}))(Y(t_i) - Y(t_{i-1}))$. For Brownian motions $(B_1, B_2)$ with correlation $\rho$, we have $E[B_1(t)B_2(t)] = \rho t$.

Reasoning: The increments $\Delta B_1 = B_1(t_i) - B_1(t_{i-1})$ and $\Delta B_2 = B_2(t_i) - B_2(t_{i-1})$ have covariance $\rho(t_i - t_{i-1})$. When we sum the products and take the limit, we get $[B_1, B_2]_t = \rho t$. This is often written informally as $dB_1 \cdot dB_2 = \rho \, dt$.

Answer: The quadratic covariation is $[B_1, B_2]_t = \rho t$.
