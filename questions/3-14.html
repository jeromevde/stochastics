The change of numeraire toolkit: when switching from numeraire S to U, the drift change involves:
Definitions: A numeraire is a reference asset used to measure relative prices. When changing from numeraire $S$ to numeraire $U$, the probability measure changes, which affects the drift of processes. The change of numeraire formula quantifies this drift adjustment.

Reasoning: When switching numeraires from $S$ to $U$, the drift of a process $X_i$ changes according to: $d\mu_i^U = d\mu_i^S - d\langle \ln X_i, \ln(S/U) \rangle$. This formula comes from Girsanov's theorem applied to the Radon-Nikodym derivative relating the two measures. The quadratic covariation term captures how the log-process co-varies with the log-ratio of numeraires, determining the drift correction needed.

Answer: The drift change involves subtracting $d\langle \ln X_i, \ln(S/U) \rangle$.
