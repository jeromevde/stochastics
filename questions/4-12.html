Hull-White's model extends Vasicek by replacing $\theta$ with $\theta$(t) to:
Definitions: The Hull-White model (also called the extended Vasicek model) has the form $dr = [\theta(t) - \kappa r] dt + \sigma dB$, where $\theta(t)$ is now time-dependent. This is a one-factor model with time-varying mean reversion level.

Reasoning: The key motivation is market consistency: the Vasicek model with constant $\theta$ typically does not match the current observed yield curve $P^{\text{market}}(0,T)$. By making $\theta(t)$ time-dependent, Hull-White can calibrate $\theta(t)$ so that the model-implied bond prices $P^{\text{model}}(0,T)$ exactly match market prices at time 0. This ensures the model is arbitrage-free relative to the current market and is particularly useful for pricing interest rate derivatives consistently.

Answer: The time-dependent $\theta(t)$ allows the model to match the market's current zero-coupon bond prices.
