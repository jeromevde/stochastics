The Black-Scholes implied volatility $\Sigma$(T,K) is the volatility that:
Definitions: Implied volatility is the volatility parameter $\sigma$ that, when input into the Black-Scholes formula, produces a theoretical price equal to the observed market price of an option. It's extracted by inverting the Black-Scholes formula. Reasoning: Given market price $C_{\text{market}}$ for a call with strike $K$ and maturity $T$, the implied volatility $\Sigma(T, K)$ solves: $C_{BS}(S_0, K, T, r, \Sigma(T, K)) = C_{\text{market}}$. This inversion is typically done numerically (e.g., Newton-Raphson). Implied volatility represents the market's expectation of future volatility and allows comparison of options across different strikes and maturities. Answer: Implied volatility makes the Black-Scholes formula match the observed market call price.
