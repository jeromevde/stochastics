Definitions: Implied volatility is the volatility parameter $\sigma$ that, when input into the Black-Scholes formula, produces a theoretical price equal to the observed market price of an option. It's extracted by inverting the Black-Scholes formula, converting a price into a volatility measure. Reasoning: Given market price $C_{\text{market}}$ for a call with strike $K$ and maturity $T$, the implied volatility $\Sigma(T, K)$ solves: $$C_{BS}(S_0, K, T, r, \Sigma(T, K)) = C_{\text{market}}$$ This inversion is typically done numerically using Newton-Raphson method with vega (the derivative of option price with respect to volatility) as the Jacobian: $$\Sigma_{n+1} = \Sigma_n - \frac{C_{BS}(\Sigma_n) - C_{\text{market}}}{\nu(\Sigma_n)}$$ where $\nu = \frac{\partial C_{BS}}{\partial \sigma}$ is vega. Implied volatility represents the market's expectation of future realized volatility and allows comparison of options across different strikes and maturities. The collection $\Sigma(T,K)$ for various strikes and maturities forms the volatility surface. Answer: Implied volatility makes the Black-Scholes formula match the observed market call price. Practical Use Case: Implied volatility is the universal language of options trading. Instead of quoting prices directly, traders quote implied volatility, making options comparable across strikes and maturities. When a trader says "SPX 1-month at-the-money vol is 15%", they're communicating more information than a dollar price. Volatility surfaces are the primary tools for trading: dealers monitor the "smile" (volatility varying by strike) and "term structure" (volatility varying by maturity). For risk management, traders compute sensitivities in "vol space"â€”vega exposure measures P&L changes per 1% volatility move. Market makers arbitrage implied volatility against realized volatility, betting that options are over/underpriced relative to actual future stock movements. Volatility indices like VIX are computed from implied volatilities of multiple strikes, serving as market fear gauges.
