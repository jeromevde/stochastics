Itô's lemma for f(B(t), t) gives df = $f_t$ dt + $f_x$ dB + ?
Definitions: Itô's lemma is the chain rule for stochastic calculus. For a function $f(x, t)$ applied to a stochastic process, it accounts for both the drift and diffusion, plus a correction term due to quadratic variation. Reasoning: The classical chain rule would give $df = f_t \, dt + f_x \, dB$. However, in stochastic calculus, we must expand to second order because $(dB)^2 = dt$ (not zero!). The second-order term from the Taylor expansion is $\frac{1}{2}f_{xx}(dB)^2 = \frac{1}{2}f_{xx} \, dt$. This extra term captures the effect of Brownian motion's quadratic variation. Answer: The missing term in Itô's lemma is $\frac{1}{2}f_{xx} \, dt$.
