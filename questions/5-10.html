Definitions: The Black formula (also called Black-76) prices European options on forwards or futures. For caplets, it prices options on forward LIBOR rates. The key requirement is that the underlying follows a lognormal distribution.

Reasoning: Under the $T_2$-forward measure $\mathbb{Q}^{T_2}$, the forward rate $F(t,T_1,T_2)$ is a martingale with dynamics $dF = \sigma F \, dW^{\mathbb{Q}^{T_2}}$. This is a driftless geometric Brownian motion, making $F(T_1)$ lognormally distributed. The caplet payoff $(T_2-T_1)\max(0, F(T_1,T_1,T_2) - K)$ can then be priced using the Black formula, analogous to Black-Scholes but with zero drift.
