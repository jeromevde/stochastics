Definitions: A semi-ring is a collection of sets closed under finite intersections, where set differences decompose into finite disjoint unions. A pre-measure on a semi-ring satisfies countable additivity on disjoint unions. Carathéodory's extension theorem extends this to a full measure on a $\sigma$-algebra. Reasoning: Starting with a pre-measure $\mu_0$ on a semi-ring $\mathcal{S}$, the theorem constructs a unique measure $\mu$ on the generated $\sigma$-algebra $\sigma(\mathcal{S})$ that agrees with $\mu_0$ on $\mathcal{S}$.
