E[(1/T)$\int_0$ᵀ $\sigma^2$(t) dt] in the Heston model equals:
Definitions: The expected average variance over $[0,T]$ is $E\left[\frac{1}{T}\int_0^T v(t) \, dt \mid v(0)\right]$ where $v = \sigma^2$ follows the CIR process in Heston. This quantity determines long-term option prices. Reasoning: From the CIR dynamics, we know $E[v(t) | v(0)] = \theta + (v(0) - \theta)e^{-\kappa t}$. Integrating and dividing by $T$: $E\left[\frac{1}{T}\int_0^T v(t) \, dt\right] = \frac{1}{T}\int_0^T [\theta + (v(0) - \theta)e^{-\kappa t}] dt = \theta + (v(0) - \theta)\frac{1 - e^{-\kappa T}}{\kappa T}$. As $T \to \infty$, the term $(1-e^{-\kappa T})/(\kappa T) \to 0$, so the expected average converges to $\theta$. Answer: $\theta + (v(0) - \theta)(1 - e^{-\kappa T})/(\kappa T)$.
