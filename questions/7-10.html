In the VG model for equity log-returns, setting $\theta$ < 0 produces:
Definitions: In the VG model, the parameter $\theta$ controls the drift component of the time-changed Brownian motion. Since log-returns follow $X(t) = \theta \Gamma_\nu(t) + \sigma B(\Gamma_\nu(t))$, the sign of $\theta$ determines the skewness of the return distribution. Reasoning: When $\theta < 0$, the drift component consistently pulls returns downward, while the random time change $\Gamma_\nu(t)$ introduces variability. This creates negative skewness: the left tail (large negative returns) becomes fatter than the right tail. This matches empirical equity return distributions and produces the volatility skew observed in equity option markets, where OTM puts trade at higher implied volatilities than OTM calls. Answer: Fatter left tails / volatility skew.
