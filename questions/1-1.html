Which type of scenarios is used to compute counterparty credit risk for OTC transactions?
Definitions: Counterparty credit risk (CCR) is the risk that a counterparty in an OTC transaction defaults before the contract matures. Real-world scenarios use actual (physical) probabilities, while risk-neutral scenarios use adjusted probabilities for no-arbitrage pricing. Reasoning: CCR involves estimating future exposure and potential losses if the counterparty defaults. Since we care about actual default probabilities (not pricing), we need scenarios whose probabilities match real-world expectations. Risk-neutral scenarios are used for derivative pricing, not risk measurement. Answer: Real-world scenarios are required because CCR deals with actual default risk under physical probabilities.
