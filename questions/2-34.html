If B(t) is standard Brownian motion, what is E[$\int_0^t$ B(s) dB(s)]?
Definitions: The Itô integral $\int_0^t B(s) \, dB(s)$ is a stochastic integral of an adapted process against Brownian motion. A key property of Itô integrals is that they are martingales (when the integrand satisfies integrability conditions).

Reasoning: Since the Itô integral is a martingale starting at 0, its expectation at any time is $E[\int_0^t B(s) \, dB(s)] = 0$. Alternatively, we know from earlier that $\int_0^t B(s) \, dB(s) = \frac{1}{2}(B(t)^2 - t)$, so $E[\int_0^t B(s) \, dB(s)] = \frac{1}{2}(E[B(t)^2] - t) = \frac{1}{2}(t - t) = 0$.

Answer: The expectation is 0.
