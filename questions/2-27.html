Definitions: A semimartingale is a stochastic process that can be decomposed into the sum of a local martingale (unpredictable, zero-drift component) and a finite-variation process (predictable, drift-like component). Càdlàg means right-continuous with left limits. Reasoning: The semimartingale decomposition $X = M + A$ separates the "random fluctuation" part $M$ from the "systematic drift" part $A$. This is the most general class for which stochastic integration is well-defined. It includes Itô processes, Brownian motion, Poisson processes, and many others used in financial modeling. Answer: A semimartingale is the sum of a (local) martingale and a càdlàg adapted finite-variation process.
