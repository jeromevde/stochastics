The forward rate F(t,$T_1$,$T_2$) in terms of zero-coupon bonds is:
Definitions: The simply-compounded forward rate $F(t,T_1,T_2)$ is the fixed rate agreed at time $t$ for borrowing/lending over the future period $[T_1, T_2]$. It can be derived from current zero-coupon bond prices $P(t,T)$. Reasoning: Consider investing in a $T_1$-bond and shorting $(P(t,T_1)/P(t,T_2))$ units of a $T_2$-bond. At $T_1$, you receive 1 unit. At $T_2$, you owe $P(t,T_1)/P(t,T_2)$ units. The implied simply-compounded rate over $[T_1,T_2]$ is: $$1 + F(t,T_1,T_2)(T_2-T_1) = \frac{P(t,T_1)}{P(t,T_2)}$$ which gives $$F(t,T_1,T_2) = \frac{P(t,T_1)/P(t,T_2) - 1}{T_2 - T_1}$$ Answer: $$F(t,T_1,T_2) = \frac{P(t,T_1)/P(t,T_2) - 1}{T_2 - T_1}$$
