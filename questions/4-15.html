Definitions: Consider a modified model with volatility proportional to $r$ (not $\sqrt{r}$): $dr = \kappa(\theta - r) dt + \sigma r \, dB$. The bank account value is $B(t) = \exp(\int_0^t r(s) \, ds)$. Reasoning: With volatility $\sigma r$, rates can grow exponentially without bound along certain paths. The tails of the distribution become so heavy that $E[\exp(\int_0^t r(s) \, ds)] = \infty$ for any $t > 0$. This makes the model economically unrealistic - it assigns infinite value to bonds, and extreme rate paths dominate expectations. This demonstrates why CIR uses $\sqrt{r}$ instead of $r$. Answer: The expectation is infinite (bank account explodes).
