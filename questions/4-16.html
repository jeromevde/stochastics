Definitions: The CIR++ model extends CIR analogously to how Hull-White extends Vasicek. The model is $r(t) = x(t) + \varphi(t)$, where $x(t)$ follows a CIR process and $\varphi(t)$ is a deterministic shift function. Reasoning: The pure CIR model typically doesn't match the current market yield curve. By adding a time-dependent shift $\varphi(t) = f^{\text{market}}(0,t) - f^{\text{CIR}}(0,t)$, where $f$ denotes instantaneous forward rates, the model can be calibrated to reproduce observed bond prices $P^{\text{market}}(0,T)$ at time 0. This ensures market consistency while preserving CIR's positive-rate property and mean-reverting dynamics. Answer: The shift $\varphi(t)$ is chosen to match market bond prices.
