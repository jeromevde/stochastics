Brownian motion has which of these properties?
Definitions: A function has bounded variation on an interval if the total absolute change along any partition is bounded. A function is differentiable at a point if its derivative exists there. Brownian motion is continuous but highly irregular.

Reasoning: Despite being continuous everywhere, Brownian motion paths are almost surely nowhere differentiableâ€”they have "corners" everywhere. This extreme roughness also implies unbounded variation on any non-trivial interval. The quadratic variation being $t$ (not zero) is another manifestation of this roughness. These properties make standard calculus inapplicable, necessitating stochastic calculus.

Answer: Brownian motion has almost surely nowhere differentiable paths.
