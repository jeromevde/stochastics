The swap numeraire C^{$\alpha,\beta$}(t) equals:
Definitions: The swap numeraire (or swap measure numeraire) $C^{\alpha,\beta}(t)$ is the present value of receiving $\tau_j$ dollars at each payment date $T_j$ in the swap. It's used as the numeraire for pricing swaptions. Reasoning: The swap numeraire is $C^{\alpha,\beta}(t) = \sum_{j=\alpha+1}^{\beta} \tau_j P(t,T_j)$, which represents the fixed-leg annuity factor. This is a portfolio of zero-coupon bonds (each bond scaled by $\tau_j$), making it a tradable asset and thus a valid numeraire. Under the corresponding swap measure, the swap rate becomes a martingale, which simplifies swaption pricing. Answer: $C^{\alpha,\beta}(t) = \sum_{j=\alpha+1}^{\beta} P(t,T_j)\tau_j$ (the fixed-leg annuity). Practical Use Case: This concept is essential for modern financial engineering and quantitative trading. Practitioners apply these principles in derivatives pricing, risk management, and portfolio optimization across asset classes including equities, fixed income, commodities, and FX markets. Understanding this material enables quants to build robust pricing models, traders to identify arbitrage opportunities, and risk managers to compute accurate hedging strategies for complex derivative portfolios.
