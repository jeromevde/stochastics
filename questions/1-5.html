Value at Risk (VaR) measures:
Definitions: Value at Risk (VaR) is a risk metric that quantifies the potential loss on a portfolio over a specified holding period at a given confidence level. For example, a 95% 1-day VaR of \$1 million means there is a 5% chance the portfolio will lose more than \$1 million in one day.

Reasoning: VaR does not measure expected profit (option A), the absolute maximum loss (option B), or standard deviation (option D). Instead, it captures the loss threshold that will not be exceeded with a specified probability (e.g., 95% or 99%). It combines three elements: holding period (e.g., 1 day), confidence level (e.g., 95%), and the loss amount.

Answer: VaR measures the potential loss over a holding period within a given confidence level, making it a percentile-based risk measure.
