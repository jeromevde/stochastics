The Black-Scholes call price formula C = $S_0$N($d_+$) − Ke^{−rT}N($d_-$) uses N($\cdot$) which is:
Definitions: The Black-Scholes formula prices European call options analytically. The function $N(\cdot)$ appearing in the formula represents the cumulative distribution function (CDF) of the standard normal distribution: $N(x) = \int_{-\infty}^x \frac{1}{\sqrt{2\pi}} e^{-z^2/2} \, dz$.

Reasoning: The formula $C = S_0 N(d_+) - K e^{-rT} N(d_-)$ arises from computing $E^\mathbb{Q}[e^{-rT}(S(T) - K)^+]$. Since $\ln(S(T))$ is normally distributed, the expectation involves integrals over the normal distribution. The $N(d_+)$ and $N(d_-)$ terms represent probabilities of exercise and discounted payoffs, computed using the standard normal CDF.

Answer: $N(\cdot)$ is the standard normal cumulative distribution function (CDF).
