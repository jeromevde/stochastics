For a deterministic function f(s), $\int_0^t$ f(s) dB(s) has distribution:
Definitions: When integrating a deterministic function $f(s)$ against Brownian motion, the result is a normally distributed random variable. The Itô isometry determines the variance.

Reasoning: The stochastic integral $\int_0^t f(s) \, dB(s)$ is a weighted sum of independent normal increments, hence Gaussian. Its expectation is zero (since the integral is a martingale starting at 0). By the Itô isometry, its variance is $\int_0^t f(s)^2 \, ds$. Therefore, the distribution is $N\left(0, \int_0^t f(s)^2 \, ds\right)$.

Answer: The distribution is $N\left(0, \int_0^t f(s)^2 \, ds\right)$.
