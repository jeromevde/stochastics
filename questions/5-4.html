The LIBOR market model specifies each forward rate $F_k$(t) under its own forward measure as:
Definitions: The LIBOR Market Model (LMM), also called the Brace-Gatarek-Musiela (BGM) model, models each forward rate $F_k(t)$ as a lognormal process under its own forward measure. This ensures positive rates and analytical caplet pricing.

Reasoning: Under the forward measure $\mathbb{Q}^{T_{k+1}}$ corresponding to forward rate $F_k$, we have $dF_k/F_k = \sigma_k(t) \, dB_k$, where $B_k$ is a Brownian motion under that measure and there is no drift term. This driftless lognormal specification ensures: (1) $F_k$ is a martingale under its own measure, (2) $F_k$ stays positive, and (3) caplets can be priced using the Black formula, consistent with market practice.

Answer: Under its own forward measure, $dF_k = \sigma_k F_k \, dB_k$ (driftless lognormal).
