Conceptually, why is the swap rate a weighted average of forward rates under the swap annuity numeraire?
Definitions: The swap annuity is $A(t)=\sum_j \tau_j P(t,T_j)$, the PV of unit fixed-leg cashflows. Reasoning: Writing floating and fixed legs on the same discounting basis naturally introduces weights proportional to each fixed payment PV contribution, i.e. $\tau_j P(t,T_j)$. Answer: The swap rate is annuity-weighted because each period contributes according to $\tau_j P(t,T_j)$. Practical Use Case: This weighting is central for mapping quoted swaption volatilities into model inputs in LMM/annuity-measure frameworks.
