Almost sure convergence implies:
Definitions: Almost sure (a.s.) convergence means $X_n \to X$ on a set of probability 1. Convergence in probability means $P(|X_n - X| > \epsilon) \to 0$ for any $\epsilon > 0$. Mean square convergence means $E[(X_n - X)^2] \to 0$.

Reasoning: The hierarchy of convergence modes is: mean square $\Rightarrow$ convergence in probability $\Leftarrow$ a.s. convergence, and all imply convergence in distribution. Almost sure convergence is pathwise convergence (except on a null set), which is stronger than probabilistic convergence but doesn't control moments. You can have a.s. convergence where tails get heavier, preventing mean square convergence.

Answer: Almost sure convergence implies convergence in probability.
