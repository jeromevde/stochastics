The SDE for Geometric Brownian Motion (GBM) is:
Definitions: Geometric Brownian Motion (GBM) is a continuous-time stochastic process where the logarithm of the variable follows a Brownian motion with drift. It's the standard model for stock prices in the Black-Scholes framework.

Reasoning: The GBM SDE is $dS = \mu S \, dt + \sigma S \, dB$, where both the drift and diffusion terms are proportional to the current value $S$. This proportionality ensures that: (1) $S$ remains positive if it starts positive, (2) returns are scale-independent, and (3) the log-returns are normally distributed. The percentage changes $dS/S = \mu \, dt + \sigma \, dB$ don't depend on the level of $S$.

Answer: The GBM SDE is $dS = \mu S \, dt + \sigma S \, dB$.
