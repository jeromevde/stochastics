Which modeling distinction best explains why GBM is common for equities while Vasicek/CIR are used for short rates?
Definitions: GBM has multiplicative dynamics and no mean-reversion. Vasicek/CIR are short-rate models built around pull toward a long-run level. Reasoning: Equity spot levels are usually treated as trend + diffusion with scale proportional to level, whereas short rates empirically display reversion toward policy/macro regimes. Answer: The key difference is proportional growth (GBM) versus mean reversion (Vasicek/CIR). Practical Use Case: Desk-level systems often mix models: GBM-like equity underlyings with affine short-rate dynamics for discounting/IR derivatives.
