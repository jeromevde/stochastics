Definitions: A complete market is one in which every contingent claim (derivative payoff) can be replicated by a self-financing trading strategy. The equivalent martingale measure is the risk-neutral probability measure used for pricing. Reasoning: The Second Fundamental Theorem of Asset Pricing (2nd FTAP) states that (assuming no arbitrage) a market is complete if and only if the equivalent martingale measure is unique. In incomplete markets, there are infinitely many EMMs, leading to a range of possible prices for non-replicable claims. Uniqueness of the EMM ensures unique prices for all derivatives. Answer: A market is complete if and only if there exists exactly one equivalent martingale measure.
