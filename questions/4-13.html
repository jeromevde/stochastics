Definitions: The Cox-Ingersoll-Ross (CIR) model is $dr = \kappa(\theta - r) dt + \sigma \sqrt{r} \, dB$, where the volatility term $\sigma\sqrt{r}$ is proportional to the square root of the rate. This ensures rates remain non-negative under certain conditions. Reasoning: The Feller condition $2\kappa\theta > \sigma^2$ ensures that $r(t)$ stays strictly positive. The $\sqrt{r}$ volatility term shrinks as $r \to 0$, which prevents the rate from becoming negative. When the condition is satisfied, the drift toward $\theta$ dominates near zero, bouncing the rate back up before it can cross zero. This is a major improvement over Vasicek. Answer: Rates remain positive if $2\kappa\theta > \sigma^2$ (the Feller condition).
