The put-call parity implies that the implied volatility of a call equals:
Definitions: Put-call parity is a fundamental no-arbitrage relationship: $C(K,T) - P(K,T) = S_0 - Ke^{-rT}$, valid for any European options with the same strike $K$ and maturity $T$. This holds regardless of the underlying model. Reasoning: Since put-call parity is model-independent and depends only on no-arbitrage, the market prices $C$ and $P$ must satisfy it exactly. When we extract implied volatilities by inverting the Black-Scholes formula for both the call and put, we must obtain the same volatility. If they differed, the put-call parity relationship would be violated for Black-Scholes prices. Therefore, calls and puts with the same strike and maturity have identical implied volatilities. Answer: The implied volatility of a put with same strike and maturity.
