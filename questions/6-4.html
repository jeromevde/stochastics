The Heston model has how many parameters to calibrate (for a risk-neutral model)?
Definitions: The Heston model for risk-neutral pricing has dynamics: $dS = rS \, dt + \sqrt{v} S \, dW_S$ and $dv = \kappa(\theta - v) dt + \xi\sqrt{v} \, dW_v$ with $dW_S \cdot dW_v = \rho \, dt$. Reasoning: The model requires specification of five parameters: (1) $\kappa$ - mean-reversion speed of variance, (2) $\theta$ - long-run variance level, (3) $\xi$ - volatility of volatility (vol-of-vol), (4) $\rho$ - correlation between price and variance shocks, and (5) $v_0 = \sigma^2(0)$ - initial variance. The drift $\mu$ is fixed at the risk-free rate $r$ by risk-neutrality. These 5 parameters are typically calibrated to market option prices. Answer: The Heston model has 5 parameters to calibrate. Practical Use Case: This concept is essential for modern financial engineering and quantitative trading. Practitioners apply these principles in derivatives pricing, risk management, and portfolio optimization across asset classes including equities, fixed income, commodities, and FX markets. Understanding this material enables quants to build robust pricing models, traders to identify arbitrage opportunities, and risk managers to compute accurate hedging strategies for complex derivative portfolios.
