In the Vasicek model, r(t)|r(s) has distribution:
Definitions: The conditional distribution of $r(t)$ given $r(s)$ describes how the short rate evolves over time in the Vasicek model. Since the SDE is linear in $r$ with Gaussian noise, the solution remains Gaussian.

Reasoning: The Vasicek SDE can be solved explicitly using an integrating factor. The solution is: $r(t) | r(s) \sim N\left(\theta + (r(s) - \theta)e^{-\kappa(t-s)}, \frac{\sigma^2}{2\kappa}(1 - e^{-2\kappa(t-s)})\right)$. The mean shows exponential decay toward $\theta$, and the variance increases from 0 (at $t=s$) to the stationary variance $\sigma^2/(2\kappa)$ as $t \to \infty$. Being Gaussian is a key feature but also means rates can go negative.

Answer: The conditional distribution is Normal.
