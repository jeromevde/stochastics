Definitions: In the Vasicek model, bond pricing requires computing the distribution of the integrated short rate $\int_0^T r(u) \, du$. Since $r$ is Gaussian, this integral is also Gaussian, and we need its variance conditional on $r(0)$.

Reasoning: The variance formula is $V(0,T) = \frac{\sigma^2}{\kappa^2}\left(T - B(0,T) - \frac{\kappa B(0,T)^2}{2}\right)$, where $B(0,T) = \frac{1}{\kappa}(1 - e^{-\kappa T})$ depends on $\kappa$ and $T$. Notably, the variance depends on the volatility $\sigma$, mean-reversion speed $\kappa$, and maturity $T$, but not on the long-term mean $\theta$ or initial rate $r(0)$.

Answer: The variance depends on $\sigma$, $\kappa$, and $T$.
