If S(1) has three possible values {2, 1, $\tfrac{1}{2}$} in a 1-period model with S(0) = 1 and r = 0, the model is:
Definitions: Market completeness requires that the number of linearly independent traded assets equals the number of states. In a complete market, every contingent claim can be replicated. Reasoning: With 3 possible outcomes and 2 assets (stock and risk-free bond), we have more states than assets (3 > 2), making the market incomplete. There exist infinitely many probability measures $(q_1, q_2, q_3)$ with $q_i > 0$, $\sum q_i = 1$, satisfying $1 = 2q_1 + 1 \cdot q_2 + \frac{1}{2}q_3$. Each such measure is an equivalent martingale measure, so by the 2nd FTAP, the market is incomplete. Answer: The model is incomplete with multiple risk-neutral measures.
