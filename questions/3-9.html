Definitions: The Fokker-Planck equation (also called the forward Kolmogorov equation) is a PDE that describes how the probability density $\rho(x,t)$ of a stochastic process evolves over time. Reasoning: For a process $dX = \mu(x,t) dt + \sigma(x,t) dB$, the probability density satisfies: $\frac{\partial \rho}{\partial t} = -\frac{\partial(\mu \rho)}{\partial x} + \frac{1}{2}\frac{\partial^2(\sigma^2 \rho)}{\partial x^2}$. This is the "forward" equation (time goes forward), complementing the backward Kolmogorov equation (Feynman-Kac). It's the probabilistic analog of a diffusion-advection equation. Answer: The Fokker-Planck equation describes the evolution of the transition probability density.
