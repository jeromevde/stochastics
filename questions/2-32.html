The reflection principle for Brownian motion states that P(sup_{0$\leqs\leq$t} B(s) $\geq$ b) equals (for b $\geq$ 0):
Definitions: The reflection principle is a key property of Brownian motion relating the maximum process $M_t = \sup_{0 \leq s \leq t} B(s)$ to the terminal value $B(t)$. It exploits the symmetry of Brownian motion.

Reasoning: If the maximum exceeds $b$, the process must have crossed level $b$ at some first passage time $\tau$. By the strong Markov property and symmetry, the path after $\tau$ is equally likely to go up or down. The event $\{M_t \geq b\}$ includes all paths with $B(t) \geq b$ plus those that crossed $b$ and came back down. By symmetry, this doubles the probability: $P(M_t \geq b) = 2P(B(t) \geq b)$.

Answer: The reflection principle gives $P(\sup_{0 \leq s \leq t} B(s) \geq b) = 2P(B(t) \geq b)$ for $b \geq 0$.
