Definitions: Girsanov's theorem describes how Brownian motion transforms under a change of measure. Given a measure $\mathbb{Q}$ defined via Radon-Nikodym derivative $d\mathbb{Q}/d\mathbb{P} = \mathcal{E}(X)$, the theorem tells us how processes change their behavior.

Reasoning: Under Girsanov's theorem, if $Y^P$ is $\mathbb{P}$-Brownian motion and we change to measure $\mathbb{Q}$ with $d\mathbb{Q}/d\mathbb{P} = \mathcal{E}(X)$, then $Y^Q_t = Y^P_t - [X, Y^P]_t$ is $\mathbb{Q}$-Brownian motion. Intuitively, the measure change adds/removes drift, and the covariation $[X, Y^P]$ quantifies this drift change. This is the foundation for risk-neutral pricing.

Answer: Under $\mathbb{Q}$, the process $Y^Q = Y^P - [X, Y^P]_t$ is $\mathbb{Q}$-Brownian motion.
