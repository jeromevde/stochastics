Definitions: Different evaluation points in the stochastic integral lead to different integral conventions. The parameter $a \in [0,1]$ determines where in each interval $[t_{i-1}, t_i]$ we evaluate: $a=0$ is left endpoint (Itô), $a=1/2$ is midpoint (Stratonovich), $a=1$ is right endpoint. Reasoning: The general formula for the integral with parameter $a$ is $\frac{1}{2}(B(t)^2 - t) + a \cdot t$. For $a = 1/2$ (Stratonovich integral), this becomes $\frac{1}{2}B(t)^2 - \frac{t}{2} + \frac{t}{2} = \frac{1}{2}B(t)^2$, which matches the ordinary calculus result. This is why Stratonovich integrals follow classical chain rules. Answer: Using the midpoint rule gives $\frac{1}{2}B(t)^2$.
