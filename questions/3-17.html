The numeraire in a market model is:
Definitions: A numeraire is a reference asset used as the unit of account in which other assets are measured. It must be a positive, adapted stochastic process representing a tradable asset (e.g., you can buy and sell it in the market). Reasoning: Common numeraires include the risk-free bank account $B(t) = e^{rt}$, zero-coupon bonds $P(t,T)$, or even a stock. When we express all prices relative to a numeraire $N(t)$ and use the associated measure, the ratios $S(t)/N(t)$ become martingales. Different numeraires lead to different but equivalent probability measures, each useful for different pricing problems. Answer: A numeraire is a positive adapted stochastic process (tradable asset) used to compare values.
