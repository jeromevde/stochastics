Definitions: The Vasicek model is a one-factor short rate model: $dr = \kappa(\theta - r) dt + \sigma dB$, where $r$ is the instantaneous short rate, $\theta$ is the long-term mean level, $\kappa$ is the speed of mean reversion, and $\sigma$ is volatility.

Reasoning: The drift term $\kappa(\theta - r)$ creates mean reversion: when $r > \theta$, the drift is negative (pulls $r$ downward toward $\theta$), and when $r < \theta$, the drift is positive (pulls $r$ upward toward $\theta$). The parameter $\kappa$ controls how quickly this reversion occurs - higher $\kappa$ means faster reversion. This captures the empirical observation that interest rates tend to fluctuate around a long-term average level.

Answer: The term represents mean reversion toward $\theta$ at speed $\kappa$.
