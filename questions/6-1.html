The Heston model assumes that the variance $\sigma^2$(t) follows:
Definitions: The Heston model is a stochastic volatility model where both the stock price and its instantaneous variance are stochastic. The stock follows $dS = \mu S \, dt + \sqrt{v} S \, dW_S$, where $v = \sigma^2$ is the variance process.

Reasoning: The variance process in Heston follows $dv = \kappa(\theta - v) dt + \xi \sqrt{v} \, dW_v$, which is exactly the Cox-Ingersoll-Ross (CIR) process. The CIR specification ensures: (1) variance remains non-negative under the Feller condition $2\kappa\theta > \xi^2$, (2) variance mean-reverts to long-run level $\theta$, and (3) the "vol-of-vol" $\xi\sqrt{v}$ scales with volatility level, preventing explosions.

Answer: The variance follows a CIR (Cox-Ingersoll-Ross) process.
