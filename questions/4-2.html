The solution of GBM dS = $\mu$S dt + $\sigma$S dB is S(t) = S(0)$\cdot$exp(...):
Definitions: The solution to the GBM SDE $dS = \mu S \, dt + \sigma S \, dB$ can be found by applying Itô's lemma to $\ln(S)$. The explicit solution expresses $S(t)$ in terms of the initial value $S(0)$, the parameters, and Brownian motion.

Reasoning: Apply Itô's lemma to $f(S) = \ln(S)$: $d\ln(S) = \frac{1}{S} dS - \frac{1}{2S^2}(dS)^2 = \mu \, dt + \sigma \, dB - \frac{1}{2}\sigma^2 \, dt = (\mu - \frac{\sigma^2}{2}) dt + \sigma \, dB$. Integrating: $\ln(S(t)) - \ln(S(0)) = (\mu - \frac{\sigma^2}{2})t + \sigma B(t)$. Exponentiating gives the solution. The $-\frac{\sigma^2}{2}$ correction is the Itô term from $(dS)^2 = \sigma^2 S^2 \, dt$.

Answer: $S(t) = S(0) \exp\left((\mu - \frac{\sigma^2}{2})t + \sigma B(t)\right)$.
