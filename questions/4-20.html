In the Black-Scholes model, if $\sigma$ = 0 and r is constant, what is the call price C for $S_0$ = 100, K = 90, r = 5%, T = 1? (Round to 2 decimal places)
Definitions: In the Black-Scholes model with zero volatility ($\sigma = 0$), the stock price becomes deterministic: $S(T) = S_0 e^{rT}$. The call option payoff is $(S(T) - K)^+ = \max(S(T) - K, 0)$, discounted at rate $r$. Reasoning: With $\sigma = 0$: $S(T) = 100 e^{0.05 \cdot 1} = 100 e^{0.05} \approx 105.127$. The payoff is $\max(105.127 - 90, 0) = 15.127$. Discounting to present value: $C = e^{-0.05} \cdot 15.127 = 0.95123 \cdot 15.127 \approx 14.39$. With zero volatility, the option price is simply the discounted deterministic payoff. Answer: The call price is approximately 14.39.
