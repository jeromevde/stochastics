If [X]$_t$ = $\int_0^t \alpha$(s$)^2$ ds when $X_t$ = $\int_0^t \alpha$(s) dB(s), the Doléans-Dade exponential $\mathcal{E}(X)_t$ equals:
Definitions: For a stochastic integral $X_t = \int_0^t \alpha(s) \, dB(s)$, the quadratic variation is $[X]_t = \int_0^t \alpha(s)^2 \, ds$ (by the Itô isometry). The Doléans-Dade exponential is $\mathcal{E}(X)_t = \exp(X_t - \frac{1}{2}[X]_t)$. Reasoning: Substituting the expressions: $\mathcal{E}(X)_t = \exp\left(\int_0^t \alpha(s) \, dB(s) - \frac{1}{2}\int_0^t \alpha(s)^2 \, ds\right)$. This exponential martingale is central to Girsanov's theorem and appears as the Radon-Nikodym derivative for measure changes. It satisfies the SDE $d\mathcal{E}(X) = \mathcal{E}(X) \alpha \, dB$. Answer: $\mathcal{E}(X)_t = \exp\left(\int_0^t \alpha(s) \, dB(s) - \frac{1}{2}\int_0^t \alpha(s)^2 \, ds\right)$.
