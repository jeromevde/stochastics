If [X]$_t$ = $\int_0^t \alpha$(s$)^2$ ds when $X_t$ = $\int_0^t \alpha$(s) dB(s), the Doléans-Dade exponential $\mathcal{E}(X)_t$ equals:
$\mathcal{E}(X)_t$ = exp($X_t$ − $\tfrac{1}{2}[X]_t$) = exp($\int_0^t \alpha$(s) dB(s) − $\tfrac{1}{2}\int_0^t \alpha$(s$)^2$ ds). This is the exponential martingale used in Girsanov's theorem.
