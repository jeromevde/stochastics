For Brownian motion B(t), the increment B(t) − B(s) for s < t has distribution:
Definitions: Brownian motion $B(t)$ has independent, stationary increments: increments over disjoint time intervals are independent, and the distribution of $B(t) - B(s)$ depends only on the length $t - s$, not on the specific times $s$ and $t$. Reasoning: By the stationary increments property, $B(t) - B(s) \sim B(t-s) - B(0) = B(t-s)$. Since $B(t-s)$ is normally distributed with mean 0 and variance $t-s$, we have $B(t) - B(s) \sim N(0, t-s)$. The variance scales linearly with time, which is characteristic of diffusive processes. Answer: The increment $B(t) - B(s)$ has distribution $N(0, t-s)$.
