The tower law states that for $\sigma$-algebras $\mathcal{G}_1 \subseteq \mathcal{G}_2 \subseteq \mathcal{F}$:
The tower law: E[E[X|$\mathcal{G}_2]|\mathcal{G}_1$] = E[X|$\mathcal{G}_1$] a.s. Conditioning on less information ($\mathcal{G}_1 \subset \mathcal{G}_2$) 'smooths out' the finer conditioning.
