In an FX model, if dX/X = ($r_{\\alpha}$ − r_f)dt + $\sigma$_X dW under the domestic measure, the Radon-Nikodým derivative dP_f/d$P_{\\alpha}$ relates to:
dP_f/d$P_{\\alpha}$|_{$\mathcal{F}_t$} = Y(t) = X(t)B_f(t)/$B_{\\alpha}$(t), the FX rate adjusted by bank account ratios. This is the key to switching between domestic and foreign measures.
