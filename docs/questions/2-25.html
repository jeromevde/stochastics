For 2D Brownian motion ($B_1$, $B_2$) with correlation $\rho$, the quadratic covariation [$B_1$, $B_2$]$_t$ equals:
[$B_1$, $B_2$]$_t$ = $\rho$t, often written as d$B_1\,\cdot$d$B_2$ = $\rho$ dt. This follows from the definition of quadratic covariation and the joint normal distribution of increments.
